Community Service

Cleaning Up Trash at the Wanocksett Summer Camp

The first bit of community service that I did for MAMS was spent picking up trash around a Boy Scouts summer camp that I went to this summer. For me, this was a very meaningful experience. What I did, specifically, was pick up trash around Camp Wanocksett in New Hampshire. This made me realize how much trash people leave just laying around. This not only made me more aware of the situation but made me more likely to want it to change. This is why after I completed the service hours, I was still mindful of the trash situation at camp and did a trash sweep of the campground before I left. This service was a benefit to the community because it made the whole area cleaner and for the community of the scouts, this was a noticeable difference that simply improved the experience for everyone. It also kept the environment not only looking cleaner but kept the wildlife safe. I would recommend cleaning up a nature area to anyone that is willing to do it. This is because it not only is gratifying watching an area become cleaner because of your work, but because it also very nice knowing that other people might notice what you are doing and also think about how they can help. Overall, I did not spend much time working on this activity, so for me this was a short but meaningful activity.

Volunteering at the Wachusett Watershed Recycling Center

My second series of community service work focused on volunteering at my local recycling center. This means lots of hard work, but also a very rewarding experience. I hate to see things that took effort and energy to manufacture go to waste without any good reason, and volunteering at the recycling center allowed me to do something to help fix this. I spent the majority of my time in the styrofoam department working on breaking down styrofoam, packaging it, and moving it into a container. This is harder than one might initially guess, as the styrofoam needs to be the correct size, type, and cleanliness. This helped me realize how many products use styrofoam that don't need it, and this was a very disheartening realization after personally seeing so much of it go to waste. However, I also saw many people come that were willing to make a difference in helping the world and my district, and this always made my day.
