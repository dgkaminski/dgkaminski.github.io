Language

In the language class with Mrs. Wildfong, I learned to be immersed in French, and to build upon my four years of previous French experience. In the intermediate French group, we learned about verb tenses, vocabulary, listening comprehension, and many other aspects of the French language. Throughout each term we were asked to complete a daily vocabulary log, which for B-term ended up having more than 250 words, as well as a bi-weekly listening log. Through both of these tasks, I learned to constantly be exposed to new French material. By interacting with my fellow classmates, I also learned to speak in French to those on the same level on me, as opposed to just those that have a more advanced understanding of the language. By only being allowed to speak French in class I was able to lose my dependency on English as a way of filling in gaps in my French knowledge. By doing everything in pure French, even things like watching movies, it was sometimes even difficult to switch back to English once class was over!

Fairy Tale

This fairy tale was one of the B-term intermediate French assignments, and this allowed me to create a cohesive story in another language, which was a very useful experience. Since I was able to do this, I was able to be creative, so for my story I put a twist on a classic Russian fairy tale. In the end, I had a final product which was one of my first pieces of creative writing in another language. Writing creatively is not easy and doing so in a foreign language adds another interesting challenge to the process. It was great fun looking at other students' fairly tales and seeing what kinds of stories they could imagine.

Meme Contest

In language class, there is a annual school-wide meme competition near the end of A-term. This is a good introduction to the world of immersion French, and allows us to use our creativity while also working in French. It is a great assignment in the sense that it allows everyone to express their opinions of the classes and of the school so far in a comical way.
